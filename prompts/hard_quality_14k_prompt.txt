You are preparing a deep strategy memo for an executive operating review.
The memo must integrate product, engineering, finance, sales, security, and operations.

Scenario:
An AI company with three business units needs a 12-month operating plan.
The board demands profitable growth while preserving innovation velocity.
The company has uneven execution quality across teams and recurring planning conflicts.

Business units:
1) Consumer assistant product
2) Enterprise workflow platform
3) Model benchmarking and optimization tooling

Current state signals:
- Revenue grew modestly, but gross margin narrowed.
- GPU cost volatility increased due to ad-hoc experiments.
- Reliability metrics improved in two services, regressed in one.
- Customer support escalations are down overall but up for enterprise incidents.
- Sales pipeline value increased, but average cycle length also increased.
- Security findings are mostly medium severity, concentrated in integration surfaces.
- Hiring plans are approved, but key roles remain unfilled.
- Data quality issues caused at least two misreported KPI incidents.

Cross-team friction points:
- Product wants faster release cadence; platform reliability team wants stronger release gates.
- Sales pushes custom commitments; engineering asks for strict template-based contracts.
- Finance asks for cost envelopes by team; experimentation owners resist hard caps.
- Security requires integration reviews; partner team claims reviews delay activation.

Task:
Create a complete operating memo with the following sections:
1) Context summary (what matters and what does not)
2) Assumption ledger (explicit assumptions only)
3) Risk topology (interdependencies between top risks)
4) Portfolio allocation proposal (people, budget, compute)
5) 4-quarter roadmap with checkpoints
6) Execution governance model
7) KPI stack with leading/lagging indicators
8) Failure-mode playbook
9) Scenario analysis (base, optimistic, stress)
10) Decision package for leadership approval

Constraints:
- No external data or references.
- Every recommendation must include expected impact and measurable signal.
- Surface confidence level for each major claim.
- Distinguish hard constraints from preferences.
- Call out where short-term wins create long-term risk.

Data appendix:
- Consumer retention: day-1 strong, day-7 weak, day-30 weaker.
- Enterprise pilots convert well when reliability proof is included.
- Benchmarking workload peaks overlap with enterprise onboarding windows.
- Incident review quality improved, but runbook coverage remains partial.
- Two high-value enterprise accounts delayed payment due to procurement controls.
- Internal tooling usage is high, but ownership model is unclear.
- Team managers report planning overhead and decision latency as major productivity drains.

Output format requirements:
- Use clear headings and compact tables where useful.
- Include a final “Decisions Required This Week” section.
- Keep tone practical and non-promotional.

Now produce the full memo.
